Miso Soup

Description

Miso Soup is a traditional Japanese dish known for its delicate umami flavor and comforting warmth. At the heart of this beloved soup is miso paste, a fermented soybean product that imparts a rich, savory depth. Typically served as a starter or alongside a main meal, Miso Soup is often enhanced with a variety of ingredients such as tofu, seaweed, and scallions, creating a harmonious balance of taste and texture. Its simplicity and depth of flavor make it a soothing, nutritious choice that embodies the essence of Japanese cuisine. Whether enjoyed as a daily staple or as part of a special meal, Miso Soup offers both nourishment and a touch of culinary elegance.

Ingedients

  • 4 cups water
  • 2 teaspoons dashi granules
  • 3 tablespoons miso paste
  • 1 (8 ounce) package silken tofu, diced
  • 2 green onions, sliced diagonally into 1/2 inch pieces

Steps

  1. Combine water and dashi granules in a medium saucepan over medium-high heat; bring to a boil. Reduce heat to medium and whisk in miso paste.
  2. Stir in tofu. Separate the layers of green onions, and add them to the soup.
  3. Simmer gently for 2 to 3 minutes before serving.
